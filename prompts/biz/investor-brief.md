# Write an Investor or Acquirer Brief

**Use case:** Write a concise, data-first brief for an investor meeting, acquisition conversation, or fundraising process.
**Works with:** Claude (any version)

---

Write a 1–2 page investor brief for [COMPANY NAME].

Include:
1. **Company overview** (2–3 sentences: what it is, what it does, who it serves)
2. **Problem and solution** (the specific problem and why your solution is better)
3. **Traction** (what exists today: revenue, users, IP, partnerships, products live)
4. **Business model** (how you make money)
5. **Market size** (TAM / SAM / SOM with sources)
6. **Ask** (investment amount, use of funds, or acquisition interest)
7. **Team** (key people and relevant credentials)

Tone: data-first, no hype. No em dashes.

**Lead with what exists:**
The most common investor brief mistake is leading with vision and burying traction. Investors fund what already works. Lead with proof — what is live, what is generating revenue, what IP is filed — then describe where it goes.
