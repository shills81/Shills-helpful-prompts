# Write an AI Responsible Use Policy

**Use case:** Create a governance policy that defines how AI can and cannot be used in your company, team, or product — covering transparency, accountability, and acceptable use.
**Works with:** Claude (any version)

---

Write an AI responsible use policy for [COMPANY / TEAM / PRODUCT NAME].

- Organization type: [STARTUP / ENTERPRISE / NONPROFIT / AGENCY]
- AI systems in use: [LIST MODELS AND TOOLS — e.g., Claude, GPT-4, custom agents]
- Highest-risk use case: [WHERE AI HAS THE MOST IMPACT ON REAL PEOPLE]
- Audience: [INTERNAL TEAM POLICY / CUSTOMER-FACING / REGULATORY FILING]

The policy must address:

1. **Acceptable use** — what AI may be used for, with examples
2. **Prohibited use** — what AI may never be used for (e.g., generating deceptive content, making final decisions about people without human review, processing data without consent)
3. **Transparency requirements** — when and how to disclose AI involvement to users, customers, or the public
4. **Human oversight** — which decisions require a human in the loop and how oversight is enforced
5. **Data handling** — what user data AI systems may access, retain, and process; how it is protected
6. **Accountability** — who owns AI outputs, who is responsible when something goes wrong
7. **Vendor and model governance** — how third-party AI models are evaluated before use; what due diligence is required
8. **Incident reporting** — how employees report AI failures, misuse, or unexpected behavior
9. **Review cadence** — how often the policy is reviewed and updated as the technology evolves
10. **Enforcement** — consequences for policy violations

Output: a complete policy document with numbered sections, ready for internal adoption or external publication.

**Note on tone:**
- For internal policies: direct and operational — tell people exactly what to do
- For customer-facing: plain language — no jargon, focus on trust and transparency
- For regulatory: precise and defensible — anticipate what an auditor will ask
