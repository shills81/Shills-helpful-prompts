# Map a Competitive Landscape

**Use case:** Understand the competitive environment for a product or brand and identify a defensible position.
**Works with:** Claude (any version)

---

Map the competitive landscape for [PRODUCT CATEGORY OR MARKET].

Identify:
- Direct competitors (same solution, same customer)
- Indirect competitors (different solution, same problem)
- Adjacent tools (different problem, overlapping customer)

For each competitor: name, pricing model, target customer, key differentiator, and main weakness.

Then position [MY PRODUCT OR BRAND] against them: where is the white space? What can I own that no one else owns?

Output: comparison table + 2–3 paragraph positioning narrative.

**Positioning principle:**
The goal is not to be better than competitors on their terms — it is to occupy a position they do not hold and cannot easily copy. Find the white space, not the head-to-head fight.
