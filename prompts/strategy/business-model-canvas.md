# Write a Business Model Canvas

**Use case:** Map out all nine components of a business model before building, pitching, or stress-testing a company or product.
**Works with:** Claude (any version)

---

Fill out a Business Model Canvas for [COMPANY OR PRODUCT NAME].

All 9 blocks — be specific, not generic:
1. **Key Partners**: who do we rely on that we don't own?
2. **Key Activities**: what must we do well to deliver the value proposition?
3. **Key Resources**: what assets make this work (IP, team, infrastructure, data)?
4. **Value Propositions**: what specific problem do we solve, and for whom?
5. **Customer Relationships**: how do we acquire, retain, and grow customers?
6. **Channels**: how do customers find us and how do we deliver value?
7. **Customer Segments**: who exactly are we serving (be specific, not "enterprises")?
8. **Cost Structure**: what are the major cost drivers?
9. **Revenue Streams**: how do we make money, and how is it priced?

**Why this is worth doing before building:**
The BMC forces clarity on the revenue model. The most common startup mistake is building before answering "how does this make money and why will customers pay for it."
