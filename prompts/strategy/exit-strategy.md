# Write an Exit Strategy Narrative

**Use case:** Articulate a credible acquisition or exit thesis for a company — useful for investor conversations, board planning, or building toward a strategic sale.
**Works with:** Claude (any version)

---

Write an exit strategy narrative for [COMPANY NAME].

- Target acquirer profile: [TYPE OF BUYER, e.g. strategic acquirer in X industry / private equity / IPO]
- Valuation thesis: [WHAT DRIVES THE NUMBER — recurring revenue, IP portfolio, user base, proprietary data, distribution]
- Timeline: [TARGET YEARS TO EXIT]
- Key milestones before exit: [LIST 3–5 THINGS THAT MUST BE TRUE]
- Tone: board-ready, not speculative

**What acquirers buy:**
- Recurring revenue (predictable, contractual cash flow)
- Defensible IP (patents, trademarks, proprietary data, trade secrets)
- Documented operations (SOPs, runbooks, repeatable processes that don't depend on one person)
- Distribution (an audience, a customer base, a channel that is hard to replicate)

A business that is only valuable because of its founder is not acquirable. Build systems and IP that work without you.
